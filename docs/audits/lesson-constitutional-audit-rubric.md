# Lesson Constitutional Audit Rubric
## Scoring Framework for Evaluating AI-Generated Lessons Under the Constitution of Learning

---

## Purpose

This document defines the audit rubric used to evaluate whether an AI-generated lesson is constitutionally faithful, structurally sound, pedagogically serious, and capable of producing real understanding.

It exists because a lesson may be fluent yet weak, polished yet fragmented, or technically correct yet educationally empty.

The rubric therefore evaluates not just correctness, but ordered educational quality.

---

# Part I. Scoring Philosophy

The rubric scores lessons according to whether they:
- form understanding rather than merely present information,
- strengthen the learner’s mind,
- preserve constitutional structure,
- bind the real and the abstract,
- generate usable capability,
- and reconnect knowledge to the larger whole.

A high score means the lesson is both structurally faithful and educationally potent.
A low score means it may still contain facts, but it does not truly function as governed learning.

---

# Part II. Scoring Scale

Each category is scored on a 0–4 scale.

## 0 — Absent or failed
## 1 — Weak
## 2 — Functional
## 3 — Strong
## 4 — Excellent

---

# Part III. Audit Categories

1. Domain Placement
2. Student Current-State Modeling
3. Student Target-State Modeling
4. Transformation-of-Mind Goal
5. Why-It-Matters Framing
6. Core Definitions
7. Key Distinctions
8. Theoretical Overview
9. Abstract / Formal Layer
10. Real-World Manifestation
11. Application / Embodiment
12. Common Errors and Corrections
13. Integration with the Greater Whole
14. Capability Gained
15. Constitutional Style Fidelity

---

# Part IV. Real-Abstract Integrity Check

In addition to category scoring, every lesson must be checked for four-layer integrity.
1. Principle present?
2. Form present?
3. Manifestation present?
4. Embodiment present?

A lesson passes only when the layers are present and meaningfully connected.

---

# Part V. Audit Summary Ratings

- Constitutional Failure
- Weak but Salvageable
- Constitutionally Functional
- Constitutionally Strong
- Constitutionally Excellent

---

# Part VI. Suggested Weighting

Recommended weighting for total score:
- Domain Placement: 5%
- Current-State Modeling: 8%
- Target-State Modeling: 8%
- Transformation Goal: 8%
- Why It Matters: 5%
- Core Definitions: 8%
- Key Distinctions: 8%
- Theoretical Overview: 10%
- Abstract/Formal Layer: 8%
- Real-World Manifestation: 8%
- Application/Embodiment: 10%
- Common Errors/Corrections: 4%
- Integration with Greater Whole: 6%
- Capability Gained: 8%
- Constitutional Style Fidelity: 4%

This weighting favors actual structure and capability over polish.

---

# Part VII. Auditor Output Template

A standard audit record should include:
- lesson_id
- audit_timestamp
- scorer_type (human or automated)
- category_scores
- real_abstract_integrity_pass
- total_score
- summary_rating
- major_deficiencies
- recommended_revisions
- approval_status

---

# Part VIII. Final Audit Directive

Do not mistake fluent educational prose for educational strength.

A lesson passes only when it:
- forms understanding,
- preserves structure,
- binds theory to reality,
- produces capability,
- and reconnects the learner to the larger whole.

Anything less is instructional theater.
