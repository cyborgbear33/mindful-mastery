# Golden Lesson Spec Example Pack
## Canonical Reference Instances for Constitution-Bound Lesson Generation

---

## Purpose

This document provides a pack of canonical example lesson-spec instances that serve as reference patterns for AI lesson generation, evaluation, testing, fine-tuning, and regression checking.

These are standard-bearing examples that show what constitutionally faithful lesson design looks like across the six great domains of learning.

---

# Example 1. Self Domain
## Topic: Attention as the Gate of Learning

### Primary domain
Self

### Adjacent domains
Trivium, Integration / Theory of All

### Transformation goal
Move the learner from passive distraction to disciplined attentional self-governance.

### Why this matters
No serious learning can occur without stable attention. Attention determines what enters the mind, what is retained, and what can be brought under judgment.

### Core definitions
- **Attention**: the directed governance of mental focus toward an object, task, or question.
- **Distraction**: the loss or fragmentation of attention through competing stimuli or internal instability.

### Integration
Attention supports Self through discipline, the Trivium through clarity, the Quadrivium through formal concentration, Science through observation, and Craft through careful making.

# Example 4. Science / Analysis Domain
## Topic: Observation Versus Inference

### Primary domain
Science / Analysis

### Transformation goal
Move the learner from interpretive blur to observational discipline.

### Why this matters
Bad observation poisons the whole chain of inquiry. If observation and inference are mixed carelessly, analysis becomes unreliable.
